About Me

Dr Abdulla

I am an Assistant Professor at Jamia Millia Islamia (Central University), New Delhi, India. Previously, I served as a Research Associate at the ICAR-National Institute of Agricultural Economics and Policy Research, New Delhi.

My prior roles include Research Associate at the National Academy of Agricultural Sciences (NAAS), New Delhi, and Young Professional at the Department of Commerce, Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India.

I hold M.Phil. and Ph.D. degrees in Economics from Aligarh Muslim University (AMU), Aligarh, India. My Ph.D. dissertation focused on “Performance of Sugar Mills in India: A Comparative Study of Maharashtra and Uttar Pradesh”, and my M.Phil. dissertation explored “Efficiency of Sugar Mills in Uttar Pradesh: An Analysis”. I also earned an M.A. and B.A. (Hons.) in Economics with Mathematics and Statistics from AMU.

My research spans poverty and inequality, trade, banking, climate change, climate resilience, development economics, environmental economics, agricultural economics, agricultural marketing, value chain analysis, agriculture & allied activities, efficiency & productivity, sustainable agri-food systems, organic farming, sustainable agriculture systems, water management, and agricultural policy evaluation.
