Background &
Expertise.

With over a decade of experience, my work focuses on applying causal inference, advanced econometrics, and statistical modeling to large-scale survey and administrative data. My research translates complex datasets into actionable, evidence-based policy insights.

Currently serving as a Researcher at the V.V. Giri National Labour Institute (Ministry of Labour & Employment, GoI). Previously, I served as an Assistant Professor at Jamia Millia Islamia, and contributed to research at ICAR-NIAP, NAAS, and the Ministry of Commerce & Industry.

Core Research Pillars

Labour Economics

Exploring labour informality, wage dynamics, worker vulnerability, employment transitions, and the efficacy of social protection programs using PLFS and NSSO data.

Industrial Productivity

Conducting ASI-based productivity decomposition, estimating technological disparities, and analyzing the economics of the industrial energy transition.

Climate & Agriculture

Evaluating climate resilience, adaptation economics, and the vulnerability of agricultural systems across varying geopolitical landscapes in India.
