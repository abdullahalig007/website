About Me

Dr Abdulla

I am an Assistant Professor at Jamia Millia Islamia (Central University), New Delhi, India. Prior to joining JMI, I worked as a Research Associate at the ICAR-National Institute of Agricultural Economics and Policy Research, New Delhi.

I have also worked as a Research Associate at the National Academy of Agricultural Sciences (NAAS), New Delhi, and as a Young Professional at the Department of Commerce, Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India.

I hold M.Phil. and Ph.D. degrees in Economics from Aligarh Muslim University (AMU), Aligarh, India. My Ph.D. dissertation was “Performance of Sugar Mills in India: A Comparative Study of Maharashtra and Uttar Pradesh”, and my M.Phil. dissertation was “Efficiency of Sugar Mills in Uttar Pradesh: An Analysis”. I also earned an M.A. and B.A. (Hons.) in Economics with Mathematics and Statistics from AMU.

My research interests include poverty and inequality, trade, banking, climate change, climate resilience, development economics, environmental economics, agricultural economics, agricultural marketing, value chain analysis, agriculture & allied activities, efficiency & productivity, sustainable agri-food systems, organic farming, sustainable agriculture systems, water management, and agricultural policy evaluation.
