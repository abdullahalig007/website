34. National Statistics Office (NSO), Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation (MoSPI) — Microdata Access Portal

The NSO provides open access to microdata from its extensive surveys and censuses through its Microdata Access Portal, powered by the National Data Archive (NADA) software of the IHSN. Researchers and policymakers can browse, search, and download metadata and unit-level data for over 178 surveys.

  • Annual Survey of Industries
  • Index of Industrial Production
  • Household Consumption Expenditure
  • Economic Census
  • Enterprises Surveys
  • Periodic Labour Force Survey
  • Employment and Unemployment
  • Land and Livestock Holding Surveys
  • Other Surveys
 National Statistics Office (NSO), MoSPI Link
35. IndiaStat — Socio-economic & Electoral Data Bank (Subscription Service)

IndiaStat, managed by Datanet India Pvt. Ltd., offers a comprehensive database of socio-economic and electoral statistics. It compiles data across multiple domains and serves as a paid subscription platform for in-depth statistical analysis.

  • General & demographic statistics
  • Economic indicators & growth data
  • Agriculture & land statistics
  • Industry & infrastructure metrics
  • Health, education, and social welfare data
  • Electoral & constituency level data
  • Environmental, forest, and natural resource data
 Datanet India / IndiaStat (Subscription) Link
36. EPWRF India Time Series (EPWRFITS) — Online Time Series Database (Subscription)

EPWRFITS is a subscription-based online database developed by the EPW Research Foundation (EPWRF). It offers long-term, sector-wise time-series data on the Indian economy, with over 100,000 variables across 27 modules.

The platform provides consistent data series, metadata, visualisation tools, and downloadable datasets for researchers.

EPW Research Foundation (EPWRF) Link
37. CMIE — Centre for Monitoring Indian Economy (Subscription / Commercial)

CMIE is a leading business information and analytics company in India, established in 1976. It provides databases and analytical tools covering company performance, investment activity, household surveys, and economic trends.

  • Prowess / Prowess dx (company financials)
  • CapEx / CapEx dx (investment tracking)
  • Economic Outlook
  • States of India
  • Industry Outlook
  • Trade dx
  • Consumer Pyramids dx
  • Beliefs & Behaviour
 Centre for Monitoring Indian Economy (CMIE) Link
38. Capitaline — Indian Corporate & Financial Data Platform (Subscription)

Capitaline provides detailed financial and non-financial information on over 45,000 listed and unlisted Indian companies. It offers modular databases and analytical tools for corporate research, industry analysis, and investment insights.

  • Company financials & ratios
  • Industry aggregates & benchmarks
  • Screener / query modules
  • Corporate events & disclosures
  • Mutual fund data (“NAV” module)
  • Transfer pricing / trade data module
  • Analytical & export tools
 Capital Market Publishers / Capitaline (Subscription) Link

7. GIS Tools

Sl. No Tool Description Link
1 QGIS Open-source GIS software for mapping, spatial analysis, and visualization of agricultural data, climate patterns, and land use. Link
2 ArcGIS Comprehensive GIS platform by Esri for advanced spatial analysis, map creation, and data integration, widely used in agricultural research. Link
3 GRASS GIS Open-source GIS tool for geospatial data management, image processing, and spatial modeling, suitable for environmental and agricultural studies. Link
4 Google Earth Engine Cloud-based platform for analyzing geospatial datasets, including satellite imagery, for climate and agricultural monitoring. Link
5 R with sf/sp Packages R programming language with GIS packages (sf, sp) for spatial data analysis and visualization, ideal for econometric and agricultural studies. Link
6 GeoDa Open-source software for spatial data analysis, exploratory data analysis, and spatial regression, useful for regional agricultural studies. Link
7 GeoFlow A Stata module for generating spatial flow arcs, useful for visualizing and analyzing directional spatial relationships. Link
8 spmap (Stata) A community-contributed Stata command by Maurizio Pisati for drawing choropleth and thematic maps by merging spatial shapefile data (converted via shp2dta or mif2dta) with attribute data. Link
9 geoplot (Stata) A newer Stata command by Ben Jann for drawing maps using frames (in Stata 16+), allowing multiple layers (regions, boundaries, rivers, labels, points) to be combined flexibly. Link
10 tmap (Stata) A suite of Stata programs by Maurizio Pisati for drawing thematic maps — including choropleth, proportional symbol, dot, deviation, and label maps. Suitable for descriptive spatial visualization and regional analysis. Link
11 bimap (Stata) A Stata command for generating bivariate maps that visualize two variables simultaneously. Uses geoplot for Stata 16+ and spmap for older versions. Link
